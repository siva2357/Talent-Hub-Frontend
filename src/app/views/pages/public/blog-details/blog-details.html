How to Optimize Your Resume for ATS

Published • Oct 12, 2023

In today's competitive job market, your resume needs to do more than just impress human recruiters—it first has to get past the Applicant Tracking System (ATS). Here is how you can ensure your profile stands out to both algorithms and humans.

What is an Applicant Tracking System?

An ATS is a software application that enables the electronic handling of recruitment and hiring needs. Most modern enterprises use an ATS to automatically filter out resumes that don't meet the specific criteria of a job description.

Did you know?

Over 75% of resumes are rejected by an ATS before they are ever seen by human eyes. Proper formatting and keyword optimization are essential.

3 Strategies to Beat the ATS

1. Use Standard Formatting

While a beautifully designed resume might catch a human's eye, it can completely confuse an ATS. Stick to standard resume formats:

  • Use simple fonts (Arial, Calibri, Helvetica)
  • Avoid complex tables, columns, and graphics
  • Save your file as a standard format (usually PDF or Word doc)
2. Optimize for Keywords

Read the job description carefully and ensure that the key skills and requirements mentioned are explicitly stated in your resume. If the job asks for "Agile Development", make sure those exact words appear in your experience section.

3. Clear Headings

ATS algorithms look for standard section headings to parse your information correctly. Use common terms like:

  • Work Experience (instead of "My Journey")
  • Education (instead of "Academic Background")
  • Skills (instead of "What I Bring to the Table")

The Bottom Line

Your goal is to make the machine's job as easy as possible so it can pass your resume to a human. By keeping your formatting clean and your language aligned with the job description, you significantly increase your chances of landing an interview.
